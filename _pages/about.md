Pascal Kolkwitz-Anstötz received his Master’s degree in Political Science, Sociology, and History at [Philipps University Marburg](https://www.uni-marburg.de/en) in 2017. From 2017 to 2021, he first worked as a research assistant and, from 2019 onward, as a research associate at the Department of Political Science, Chair of Political Science Methods and Empirical Democracy Research in Marburg. Since 2021, he has been part of [GESIS](https://www.gesis.org/en/home), contributing to the BMFTR-funded project [DP-R&#124;EX](https://datenportal-rechtsextremismus.de/en/). His research focuses on political attitudes and behavior as well as political psychology, with a particular emphasis on intergroup relations in post-migrant societies. He is currently pursuing his doctorate at the Faculty of Management, Economics, and Social Sciences at the [University of Cologne](https://cgs.uni-koeln.de/en/). In his PhD project, he is investigating “Antisemitism Across Societal Groups in Germany: Manifestations, Prevalence, and Determinants”.
